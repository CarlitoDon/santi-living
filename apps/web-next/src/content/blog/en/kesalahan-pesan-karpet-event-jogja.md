Many event carpet issues occur not because the vendor is unwilling to help, but because the initial information provided is too minimal. This article discusses the most common **event carpet ordering mistakes** that lead to changes in estimates, delayed installation, or an untidy final result.

## 1. Booking at the Last Minute

Carpets require checking the type, size, fleet schedule, and location access. Last-minute bookings can be requested, but the chances of a good match are lower, especially for busy dates or distant areas.

The solution: contact the admin as soon as the event date and location are decided, even if the final measurements are still estimates.

## 2. Incorrect Area Measurements

The most common mistake is only mentioning the number of guests. However, carpet requirements are calculated based on the area: aisle length, room width, booth size, or stage area. The number of guests does not automatically indicate the required metres of carpet.

The solution: send the length x width measurements, a simple floor plan, or photos of the area from several angles.

## 3. Unconfirmed Colours and Patterns

Red carpets, rugs, or exhibition booth carpets serve different functions. For formal events, colours and patterns affect photos, guest flow, and the room's atmosphere. Do not assume all colours are always available on the event date.

The solution: share the event theme and ask about available options via WhatsApp.

## 4. Unclear Venue Access

City venues, hotels, campuses, and buildings often have loading regulations. If parking access, stairs, lifts, or dismantling times are unclear, the estimated manpower and time required may change.

The solution: send the venue rules from the start, especially for the Malioboro area, campuses, or multi-storey buildings.

## 5. Forgetting About Cleaning and Stain Risks

Outdoor events, food, rain, or large crowds can increase the risk of cleaning fees. Deposits or cleaning clauses are not a penalty; they are part of keeping the items in usable condition after the event.

The solution: explain the area conditions and event type honestly so the admin can provide clear usage guidelines.

## FAQ

**Is it okay to book without exact measurements?**
It is fine for an initial consultation, but the estimate will be more accurate once the measurements or area photos are sent.

**Does venue access affect the price?**
It can affect the logistics estimate, especially if there are stairs, distant parking, or loading hour regulations.

**Do all stains incur a cleaning fee?**
The cleaning policy needs to be confirmed. Share the risks of the event from the start to avoid any misunderstandings.
