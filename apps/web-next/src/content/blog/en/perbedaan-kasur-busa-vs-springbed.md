When looking for a **Jogja mattress rental**, you might be faced with two main options: a Foam Mattress or a Spring Mattress. Which one is better suited to your needs? Let's break down the differences so you can make the right choice.

## 1. Foam Mattress
**Advantages:**
- **Practical & Lightweight**: Very easy to move around in tight spaces.
- **Even Support**: Great for posture if using *High Density* foam, like the ones available at **Santi Living**.
- **More Economical**: Perfect for short-term or daily needs.

**Disadvantages:**
- Lacks the "bouncy" feel compared to a spring mattress.

## 2. Spring Mattress
**Advantages:**
- **Spring Effect**: Provides a luxurious and comfortable feel when moving.
- **Air Circulation**: The internal spring structure allows air to flow more freely, keeping it cooler.

**Disadvantages:**
- **Very Heavy**: Difficult to move on your own, especially if you have to carry it upstairs in a boarding house.
- **Risk of Noisy Springs**: In older units, the springs can make a squeaking sound.

## Conclusion: Which One Should You Rent?
If you need a quick, affordable, and hygienic solution for a boarding room or a minimalist home, a **High-Density Foam Mattress** is the winner. However, if you are renting for exclusive accommodation and do not need to move items frequently, a spring mattress could be the choice for you.
