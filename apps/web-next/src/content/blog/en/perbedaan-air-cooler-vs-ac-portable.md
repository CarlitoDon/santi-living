When the weather in Jogja is scorching hot, finding a room cooler is a primary need for students renting rooms or tenants in rented houses. The two most popular options to rent are **Air Coolers** and **Portable ACs**. 

Both work differently, and choosing the wrong one could lead to skyrocketing electricity bills or discomfort. Let's break down the comparison so you can make the right decision!

## 1. How They Work & Cooling Effect
*   **Portable AC**: Uses a compressor and refrigerant (just like a wall-mounted AC). The air produced is genuinely cold and dry. However, a Portable AC **must** have a large hot air exhaust hose that needs to be directed out of a window.
*   **Air Cooler**: Uses the principle of evaporation. Hot air is drawn through pads moistened with ice water (*Ice Packs*). The air blown out feels cool and humid. It is cooler than a regular fan, but it cannot cool down an entire room like an AC.

## 2. Electricity Consumption (The Most Crucial Part!)
*   **Portable AC**: Very power-hungry (usually over 800 Watts). If your rented room has limited power (e.g. 900VA), the circuit breaker will frequently trip, and your prepaid electricity bill will be very expensive.
*   **Air Cooler**: Super energy-efficient! On average, it only consumes 50 - 80 Watts. You can leave it running all night without worrying about a skyrocketing electricity bill at the end of the month.

## 3. Practicality
*   **Portable AC**: Heavy, difficult to move around, and installing the exhaust hose is quite a hassle (requires proper window access).
*   **Air Cooler**: Highly practical. Equipped with wheels, just plug it in, fill it with ice water, and it's ready to use in any corner of the room.

## Our Conclusion

If you live in a rented room with limited electricity and are looking for a practical and affordable solution, **renting an Air Cooler** is a much smarter and budget-friendly choice. You still get cool air for a good night's sleep without worrying about electricity costs.
