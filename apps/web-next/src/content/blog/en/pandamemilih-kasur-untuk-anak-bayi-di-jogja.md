# Guide to Choosing Mattresses for Children and Babies in Jogja: Comfortable, Safe, and Healthy

Choosing the right bed for children and babies is not just about comfort, but also about their developmental well-being and safety. Babies and young children require different support compared to adults because their bodies are still developing and are more sensitive to the materials and structure of the bed.

At Santi Living, we understand the importance of giving your little one a good start to their sleep. This article will cover a complete guide to choosing the right rental mattress for children and babies in Jogja.

## 1. Consider the Age and Growth Stage of the Child

### Babies (0-12 months)
- **Basic Needs**: A firm and flat surface to reduce the risk of suffocation and support a spine that is not yet fully formed.
- **Maximum Hygiene**: Babies are more vulnerable to infections and irritation because their immune systems are not yet fully functional.
- **Size**: Standard baby beds are usually 120x60 cm, but there are also mini sizes or those that fit baby cots.

### Toddlers (1-3 years)
- **Transition to a Big Bed**: Many parents move their children from a cot to a toddler mattress or a larger bed at this age.
- **Safety**: Consider a mattress with safety rails or a low-to-the-ground bed to prevent falls if an active child tends to wander out of bed.
- **Hygiene**: Still highly important as children still frequently have minor accidents, such as wetting the bed or vomiting.

### School-Aged Children (4-12 years)
- **Comfort and Support**: These children are active at school and need a bed that aids good recovery.
- **Preferences**: Children may start to express their preferences regarding the colour, theme, or type of mattress.
- **Durability**: Since children may play more actively on the bed, choose one that is durable enough.

## 2. Safe and Comfortable Bed Types for Children

### Baby Mattresses/Cots with the Right Base
- Choose a cot with a sturdy base that does not wobble when weight is applied.
- The mattress must be thick enough (at least 8-10 cm) but remain firm to provide good back support.
- Avoid cots that are too soft, which can increase the risk of entrapment or breathing difficulties for the baby.

### Small-Sized Children's Mattresses
- Generally in Single size (90x200 cm) or child-specific sizes.
- Ensure it has adequate support without being too hard.
- There are also low-profile mattress options to reduce the risk of falls.

### Mattresses with Side Protection (Guardrails)
- For children transitioning from a cot to a large mattress, guardrails can provide a sense of security and prevent nighttime falls.
- Choose ones that can be removed when no longer needed.

### Floor Beds or Montessori Beds
- An increasingly popular choice for the Montessori approach: a bed placed directly on the floor without a high frame.
- Encourages independence and safety as there is no risk of falling from a height.
- Ensure the floor base is clean, dry, and uses a rug or a sufficiently thick and comfortable mattress.

## 3. Safe Materials and Firmness for Children

### Non-Toxic and Hypoallergenic Materials
- Ensure the cot or mattress materials are free from harmful chemicals such as formaldehyde, phthalates, or brominated flame retardants.
- Choose those certified by OEKO-TEX or CertiPUR-US to guarantee safe materials.
- For sensitive babies, consider organic materials such as natural latex foam or organic wool.

### The Right Level of Firmness
- **Babies**: Very firm to support the spine and reduce the risk of sinking.
- **Toddlers and Young Children**: Medium-firm to medium, depending on weight and preference.
- Avoid mattresses that are too soft, which do not provide enough support for a developing spine.

### Resistance to Liquids and Minor Accidents
- Young children can wet the bed or vomit. Choose a cot or mattress with a removable and washable waterproof cover.
- Alternatively, use a waterproof mattress protector that you can place over the mattress.

## 4. Maximum Hygiene and Sterilisation for Children

### Easy to Wash and Sanitise
- Choose a cot or mattress with a cover that can be removed and machine-washed.
- For babies, more frequent washing is often required due to drool, vomit, or minor accidents.
- Ensure the cover material does not shrink or stretch after several washes.

### Professional Sterilisation
- At Santi Living, all our beds (including those for children) undergo professional cleaning and sterilisation between rentals, using products that are safe and effective for babies and children.
- This is crucial because a child's immune system is still developing.

### Routine Home Care
- Vacuum or dust the mattress weekly using a soft brush or a vacuum cleaner with a soft attachment.
- Mattress protectors or sheets can be washed regularly as needed.
