## Categorise Needs by Day

**D-2 or D-1.** Focus on items that must be ready before the family arrives: mattresses, bedding, fans/air coolers, and secure storage areas.

**The Wedding Day.** Focus on items that support guests and the committee: cooling units, display TVs, carpets/rugs by request, and delivery routes that do not disrupt the decoration vendors.

**D+1.** Ensure a clear collection schedule so the house or venue can be tidied up quickly. Appoint one PIC (person in charge) who is easy to contact after the event.

## How to Avoid Ordering the Wrong Items

Firstly, do not order based on a vague estimate of the crowd. Calculate who is actually staying overnight, who is just in transit, and who needs a brief rest area.

Secondly, separate the needs of the family from those of the committee/vendors. The family might need mattresses and bedding, while the committee is more likely to need a rest area, cooling units, or a display TV for internal information.

Thirdly, send photos of the area if possible. Photos of the living room, garage, spare room, or marquee/tent help the admin provide more realistic placement advice.
