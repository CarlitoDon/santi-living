## Why plan ahead?

The need for carpets, fans, air coolers, spare mattresses, and supporting equipment often arises suddenly when guests visit, events are held, or the number of occupants changes. For families, communities, and small teams hosting an open house with guests coming and going, buying new equipment is sometimes inefficient because the items are only used for a few days. Renting is a more convenient option: needs can be met when required, and the house or venue becomes spacious again once it is over.

The most common scenario is when guests arrive in stages throughout the day and the house needs to remain comfortable for sitting, resting, or waiting. In these situations, rushed decisions can lead to unsuitable items: insufficient quantities, sizes that do not fit the room, or delivery schedules that do not align with the guests' arrival. Therefore, before ordering, make a simple list containing the number of people, duration of use, location, and any additional equipment required.

## Determine the number of people and duration of use

The first step is to calculate the number of users, not just the quantity of items. For sleeping arrangements, consider who will be using the mattresses: children, adults, the elderly, or guests who need more space. For event needs, estimate the number of guests attending at the same time, rather than the total number of invitees throughout the day.

Duration is also important. Using items for one night is different from three to seven nights. The longer the duration, the more important it is to ensure the items are comfortable, easy to move, and do not disrupt daily activities. If the schedule is not yet certain, provide estimated start and end dates from the beginning so the team can help check availability.

## Check the space, access, and placement points

Before choosing items, measure the area to be used in a practical way. It does not have to be a detailed measurement, but ensure you have an idea: a narrow bedroom, living room, terrace, garage, or lodging area. For extra mattresses, think about walking paths, door positions, and access to the bathroom. For fans, air coolers, or TVs, check the power outlets and the direction they will face the users.

If the items will be used upstairs, in a narrow alley, a boarding house (kos), or a rented house with limited access, please inform us from the start. Details like this help avoid incorrect estimates during delivery. The goal is simple: the items arrive, can be placed immediately, and do not take up too much time on site.

## Choose the right package, not the largest one

Effective renting does not mean taking all items at once. For an equipment rental checklist for an open house in Jogja, start with the core needs first: carpets, fans, air coolers, spare mattresses, and supporting equipment. After that, decide on additions that are truly helpful, such as blankets for overnight guests, fans for warm rooms, or a TV for events that require a screen.

Avoid ordering just out of fear of running short. It is better to explain the situation in detail, then ask for a reasonable quantity recommendation. This way, costs, space, and the delivery process are more manageable. For families or lodging managers, this approach also helps keep the guest experience tidy without cluttering the rooms.
