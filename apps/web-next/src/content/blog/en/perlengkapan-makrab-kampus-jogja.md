## How to Divide Needs by Zone

**Committee sleeping zone.** Calculate the number of mattresses based on the number of committee members sleeping at the same time, not the total number of committee members. If there is a shift schedule, the number of mattresses can be more efficient.

**Participant zone.** If participants bring their own gear, Santi Living can focus on the committee or backups. If participants need sleeping support, the quantity and duration must be clear from the start.

**Activity zone.** For games, briefings, or discussions, consider floor mats, TV displays, and coolers according to the layout.

**Consumption and transit zone.** If this area is hot or crowded, fans/air coolers can be placed to support comfort.

## Information to Send to the Admin

- Event name and gathering dates.
- Full location: campus, villa, basecamp, house, or hall.
- Number of committee members staying overnight or on night watch.
- Estimated number of mattresses/bedding.
- Whether coolers, TV displays, or floor mats are required on request.
- Delivery and collection times.
- Location access: parking, stairs, floor level, or narrow roads.

## Safe Budget Guidelines for the Committee

The committee should not finalise figures before availability and routes are checked. Create a budget with categories: mattresses/bedding, cooling, displays, floor mats, and transport. After that, send the details to the admin to get a more accurate estimate.

For on-request items such as carpets, rugs, or TV displays, prepare backup options. This way, if a particular date is busy, the committee can still adjust the layout without changing the entire event.
