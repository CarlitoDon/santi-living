## Uses of TV Displays at Events

**Registration and event information.** TVs can display the event name, registration flow, QR codes, or session schedules so guests do not have to keep asking the organising committee.

**Sponsor loops.** For community, campus, or office events, TVs can play sponsor logos, partners, or branding materials on a loop.

**Demo booths.** Exhibition tenants can play product videos, catalogues, testimonials, or instructions for use to make the booth attract attention more easily.

**Bazaar menus and promos.** For food bazaars or MSMEs, TVs can serve as digital menu boards that can be flexibly updated according to stock and event hours.

**Internal rundown.** In the committee area, TVs can help the crew view the schedule without constantly opening group chats.

## Things to Check Before Renting a TV

First, determine the area size and viewing distance. A small booth does not always need a large screen; what is more important is that the TV position is clearly visible and does not block the flow of visitors.

Second, prepare the media source. Will the video be played from a USB flash drive, laptop, or another device? Ensure the file format, cables, and audio requirements are clear before the event.

Third, check the power outlets and cable safety. TV cables must not run across guest pathways. If the location is crowded, ask the committee to set up a secure position.

Fourth, send the loading schedule. Exhibition and bazaar events usually have strict load-in times. Delivery and collection schedules must be adjusted to the venue rules.
