## When is an Air Cooler Suitable for an Event?

**Tents or semi-outdoor areas.** Air coolers make the most sense in areas that still have air circulation, such as open tents, garages, pavilions, or large living rooms where the doors can be opened.

**Bazaar and exhibition booths.** Booth staff often stand for long periods in hot areas. Air coolers can be directed towards the work area so the crew can serve visitors more comfortably.

**Guest waiting areas.** During wedding preparations, celebrations, or family events, waiting areas are often used by many people at once. Portable coolers can help reduce the stuffiness.

**Organiser rest areas.** If the crew also requires mattresses, pillows, and blankets, read [Jogja event crew mattress hire](/artikel/sewa-kasur-crew-event-jogja).

## Limitations to Understand

An air cooler is not a permanent air conditioner. Its effect is most noticeable when directed at people or activity areas, rather than cooling an entire large building. For tightly closed rooms, the results may not be optimal as humidity increases and circulation is limited.

Organisers also need to prepare safe electrical outlets. Do not place cables in guest pathways, near wet areas, or spots where they can easily be tripped over. If the unit requires water or ice packs, assign someone to be responsible for checking the unit's condition during the event.

## Tips for More Effective Placement

Firstly, direct the airflow towards areas where people gather. An air cooler is most effective when the airflow goes directly towards seating areas, registration desks, or booth staff.

Secondly, keep cables away from walkways. Busy events require safe placement for children, the elderly, guests, and crew.

Thirdly, ensure there is circulation. For semi-outdoor areas, open part of the tent sides or room doors so the air does not feel stuffy.

Fourthly, assign someone to manage the unit. This person will help check the water/ice packs, cable positions, and the unit's condition during the event.
