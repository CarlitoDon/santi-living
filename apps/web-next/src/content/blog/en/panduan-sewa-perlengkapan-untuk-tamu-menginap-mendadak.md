## Why plan ahead?

The need for mattresses, pillows, bolsters, blankets, fans, and temporary sleeping spaces often arises suddenly when there are guests, events, or changes in the number of occupants. For families who frequently host out-of-town guests or relatives arriving at short notice, buying new equipment is sometimes inefficient as the items are only used for a few days. Renting is a more convenient option: needs can be met when required, and the house or event venue becomes spacious again once it is over.

The most common scenario is when news of overnight guests arrives on the same day and there is not enough bedding at home. In such situations, rushed decisions can lead to unsuitable items: insufficient quantities, sizes that do not fit the room, or delivery schedules that do not align with the guests' arrival. Therefore, before ordering, make a simple list containing the number of people, duration of use, location, and any additional equipment needed.

## Determine the number of people and duration of use

The first step is to calculate the number of users, not just the quantity of items. For sleeping arrangements, consider who will be using the mattresses: children, adults, the elderly, or guests who need more space. For event needs, estimate the number of guests attending at the same time, rather than the total number of invitees throughout the day.

Duration is also important. A one-night stay is different from three to seven nights. The longer the duration, the more important it is to ensure the items are comfortable, easy to move, and do not disrupt daily activities. If the schedule is not yet certain, provide estimated start and end dates from the beginning so the team can help check availability.

## Check the space, access, and placement points

Before choosing items, measure the area to be used in a practical way. It does not have to be a detailed measurement, but make sure you have an idea: a small bedroom, living room, terrace, garage, or lodging area. For extra mattresses, think about walking paths, door positions, and access to the bathroom. For fans, air coolers, or TVs, check the power sockets and the direction they will face.

If the items are to be used upstairs, in a narrow alley, a boarding house (kos), or a rented house with limited access, please inform us from the start. Details like this help avoid miscalculations during delivery. The goal is simple: once the items arrive, they can be placed immediately without taking up too much time on site.

## Choose the right package, not the largest one

Effective renting does not mean taking everything at once. For a guide to renting equipment for last-minute overnight guests, start with the core essentials first: mattresses, pillows, bolsters, blankets, fans, and temporary sleeping spaces. Only then decide on additions that will genuinely help, such as blankets for overnight guests, fans for warm rooms, or a TV for events that require a screen.

Avoid ordering just out of fear of running short. It is better to explain the situation in detail and ask for a reasonable quantity recommendation. This way, costs, space, and the delivery process remain manageable. For families or accommodation managers, this approach also helps keep the guest experience tidy without cluttering the room with items.
