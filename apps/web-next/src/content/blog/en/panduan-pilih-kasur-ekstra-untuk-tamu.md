Having extended family or old friends over to stay is a wonderful occasion. However, hosts are often left scratching their heads when it comes to sleeping arrangements. *'We have enough rooms, but not enough beds,'* is the most common complaint, especially during the holiday season or Eid in Yogyakarta.

Do you need to buy a new mattress every time you have guests? Of course not. Here is a guide to choosing a smart **extra bed** solution so your guests can sleep soundly without cluttering your home with piles of spare mattresses.

## 1. Match the Size to the Room
Before looking for a mattress, measure the available space in your bedroom or living room.
- **Single Mattress (90/100x200 cm):** The safest option to place alongside the main bed or in a corner of the room. Perfect for one adult or a young child.
- **Double Mattress (160x200 cm):** If you have a reasonably spacious living room, a double mattress is more practical if your guests are a couple.

## 2. Pay Attention to Mattress Thickness
Don't let your guests wake up with a sore back. Many cheap "roll-up mattresses" on the market are extremely thin (under 5-8 cm). For hotel-standard comfort, make sure you provide a mattress with a minimum thickness of **15 cm**.
