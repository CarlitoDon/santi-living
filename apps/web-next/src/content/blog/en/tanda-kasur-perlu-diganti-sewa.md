Renting a mattress from Santi Living is a practical solution for your sleeping needs in Jogja. However, like any other bedding, rented mattresses can also deteriorate in quality over time. Recognise the following 7 signs to ensure your sleeping comfort is always maintained.

**1. The Mattress Surface Starts to Become Uneven or Saggy**

Try running your hand across the surface of the mattress. If any part feels uneven, saggy, or lumpy — this is a sign that the internal structure of the mattress has changed. Long-term use can cause the foam or springs to compress in areas that frequently bear body weight, especially the hips and shoulders.

**2. Squeaking Noises When You Move**

A spring mattress that is starting to wear out often makes a *creaking* sound when you roll over or get up. This indicates that the springs inside the mattress are loose or broken. Besides disrupting your comfort, this condition also reduces optimal support for your spine.

**3. You Frequently Wake Up with Body Aches**

Waking up should make your body feel refreshed, not sore. If you feel pain in your neck, shoulders, or lower back every morning, it is highly likely that the mattress is no longer providing adequate support for your posture.

**4. Unexplained Allergies or Itching**

Although Santi Living always maintains the cleanliness of the mattresses, environmental factors such as dust and Jogja's typical humidity can trigger allergic reactions on a mattress that has been used for too long. If you frequently sneeze or itch in bed, please report it to our team immediately.

**5. The Edges or Corners of the Mattress Start to Sag**

Edges of the mattress that sag or do not return to their original shape are a sign that the filling material has lost its elasticity. This is not just an aesthetic issue, but it also reduces the usable area for a comfortable and safe sleep.

**6. The Mattress Feels Warmer Than Usual**

An old mattress tends to trap more heat. If you often wake up feeling hot even though the temperature in Jogja is normal, the mattress material may have lost its ability to circulate air optimally.

**7. It Has Been Used for More Than 2–3 Years**

The reasonable lifespan for optimal mattress performance is around 2–3 years. If your rental period has been quite long, you are entitled to request a fresh replacement unit without waiting for signs of damage to appear.
