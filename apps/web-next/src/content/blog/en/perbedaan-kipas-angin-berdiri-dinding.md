When the weather in Jogja is at its hottest, renting a fan is the best decision to stay comfortable. However, in the market (and also at **Santi Living**), the most frequently requested type of fan is the **Pedestal Fan (*Stand Fan*)**. 

Many people ask why we don't rent out wall-mounted or ceiling fans. Let's discuss the differences so you don't make the wrong choice.

## 1. Wall-Mounted Fans (Wall Fans)
*   **Pros**: Saves floor space (as it is mounted on the wall), and the airflow can be more evenly distributed from top to bottom.
*   **Fatal Drawback for Renting**: You **must** nail or drill into the walls of your boarding room/house to install it. This is often strictly forbidden by landlords. Additionally, you cannot move it to another room if you suddenly need it there.

## 2. Pedestal Fans (Stand Fans)
This is the star of the show in the world of **Jogja fan rentals**. 
*   **Limitless Mobility**: Today you might need it in the living room, tomorrow afternoon near your study desk, and at night next to your bed. You can easily move a *stand fan* wherever you want with just one hand.
*   **Adjustable Height**: Want to sleep on a mattress on the floor? Lower the stand. Hosting guests sitting on chairs? Raise it up. Wall-mounted fans simply don't have this flexibility.
*   **No Property Damage**: Simply place it on the floor and plug it in. Your landlord is guaranteed to be 100% happy, and there will be no complaints since no holes are drilled into the walls.

That is why **Santi Living** focuses its rental service on 16-inch *Stand Fans*. It is the perfect blend of powerful airflow and practical use, whether for students in boarding houses or for hosting events.
