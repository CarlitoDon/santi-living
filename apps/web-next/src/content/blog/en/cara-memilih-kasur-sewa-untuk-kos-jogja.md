## 5 Tips for Choosing a Rental Mattress for Your Student Room

### 1. Choose High-Density Foam (HD Foam), Not Regular Foam

Regular foam mattresses sag quickly and become uncomfortable after a few weeks. Make sure the vendor provides a **High-Density foam mattress** that:
- Does not sag easily even after months of use
- Provides good spinal support
- Feels comfortable for both sleeping and sitting

### 2. Check the Vendor's Cleaning Process

This is the most important part! Don't just rent from any vendor with an unclear cleaning process. Ask:
- Is the mattress washed before delivery?
- Is there an anti-bacterial and UV sterilisation process?
- Are the bedsheets and pillows washed as well?

**At Santi Living**, every mattress goes through 7 stages: inspection → vacuuming → anti-bacterial treatment → UV-C → drying → fragrancing → quality check.

### 3. Match the Size to Your Room

Student rooms in Jogja are generally small. Choose the right size:
- **Single (90×200cm)** — For standard student rooms, most space-saving
- **Double (120×200cm)** — For more spacious rooms or if you like to stretch out
- **Queen (160×200cm)** — For premium rooms or couples

**Tip:** Measure your room before ordering! Make sure there is at least 60cm of clearance space to walk.

### 4. Compare Prices and Services

Don't just look for the cheapest price. Also compare:
- Is there a delivery and collection service?
- Is the price inclusive of bedsheets and pillows?
- Can you easily extend the rental period?
- Are there any hidden fees?

**Comparison example:**
- Santi Living: Rp35,000/day (complete package) + free delivery and collection
- Second-hand marketplace mattress: Rp200,000-400,000 (one-off purchase, quality not guaranteed)
- Hotel: Rp200,000+/night (not for the long term)

### 5. Read Reviews and Testimonials

Before deciding, check reviews from other customers. Trusted vendors usually have:
- High ratings on their Google Business Profile
- Testimonials from real customers
- Photo evidence of clean and well-maintained mattresses
