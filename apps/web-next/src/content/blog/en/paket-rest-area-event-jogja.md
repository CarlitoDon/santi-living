## Who Needs an Event Rest Area?

**EOs (Event Organisers) and production vendors.** Production teams often work before guests arrive and are still on duty after the event ends. A rest area helps them take turns resting without having to leave the venue.

**Campus committees and communities.** Student gatherings (*makrab*), campus festivals, competitions, and community activities usually involve many volunteers. If the activities run late into the night or overnight, a rest area needs to be properly set up. For student gatherings, also read [Jogja campus student gathering equipment](/artikel/perlengkapan-makrab-kampus-jogja).

**Office gatherings and training.** Day-long office events often require a transit room for the internal team, drivers, or field organisers. See also [Jogja office gathering equipment](/artikel/perlengkapan-gathering-kantor-jogja).

**Wedding preparation.** Families, organisers, and vendors often gather from the day before (D-1). A rest area makes coordination more humane without turning the house into an equipment warehouse.

## How to Calculate the Number of Crew Mattresses

Do not simply match the number of mattresses to the number of organisers. For a rest area, mattresses are usually used in shifts. For example, 20 crew members do not necessarily need 20 mattresses if the rest schedule is rotated. Start with the number of people who actually need to sleep at the same time, then add a small buffer if the event runs overnight.

If the event lasts for several days, consider the delivery, storage, and collection schedules. Items must be placed in a secure area so they do not get mixed up with production equipment or catering.
