## Why plan ahead?

The need for pillows, bolsters, blankets, and extra mattresses often arises suddenly when there are guests, events, or changes in the number of occupants. For small organisers, families, and accommodation managers preparing many temporary beds, buying new equipment is sometimes inefficient as the items are only used for a few days. Renting is a more convenient option: needs can be met when required, and the house or venue goes back to being spacious once it is over.

The most common scenario is when mattresses are already available but the supporting bedding is not enough for everyone. In these circumstances, rushed decisions can lead to mismatched items: insufficient quantities, sizes that do not fit the room, or delivery schedules that do not align with guest arrivals. Therefore, before ordering, make a simple list containing the number of people, duration of use, location, and any additional equipment required.

## Determine the number of people and duration of use

The first step is to calculate the number of users, not just the quantity of items. For sleeping arrangements, consider who will be using the mattresses: children, adults, the elderly, or guests who need more space. For events, estimate the number of guests attending at the same time, rather than the total number of invitees throughout the day.

Duration is also important. A one-night stay is different from three to seven nights. The longer the duration, the more important it is to ensure the items are comfortable, easy to move, and do not disrupt daily activities. If the schedule is not yet finalised, provide estimated start and end dates from the outset so the team can help check availability.

## Check the space, access, and placement points

Before choosing items, measure the area to be used in a practical way. It does not have to be a detailed measurement, but make sure you have an idea: a narrow bedroom, living room, terrace, garage, or accommodation area. For extra mattresses, think about walkways, door positions, and access to the bathroom. For fans, air coolers, or TVs, check the sockets and the direction they will face.

If the items are to be used upstairs, in a narrow alley, a boarding house (kos), or a rented house with limited access, please inform us from the start. Details like this help avoid incorrect estimates during delivery. The goal is simple: the items arrive, can be placed immediately, and do not take up too much time on site.

## Choose the right package, not the largest one

Effective renting does not mean taking all items at once. To calculate the pillow and bolster requirements for a group, start with the core needs first: pillows, bolsters, blankets, and extra mattresses. Only then decide on additions that genuinely help, such as blankets for overnight guests, fans for warm rooms, or a TV for events requiring a screen.

Avoid ordering just out of fear of running short. It is better to explain the situation in detail and ask for a reasonable quantity recommendation. This keeps costs, space, and the delivery process under control. For families or accommodation managers, this approach also helps keep the guest experience tidy without cluttering the rooms.

- The number of users has been calculated.
- Start and estimated end dates are clear.
- Location and delivery access have been explained.
- Core items and additional items have been separated.
- An active WhatsApp number for coordination is ready.
