# Introduction

A mattress is where we rest after a long day of activities. However, sometimes a mattress can emit an unpleasant odour that disrupts sleep comfort. A musty smell on a mattress is usually caused by excess moisture, sweat, or liquid spills that are not cleaned up immediately.

At Santi Living, we understand the importance of keeping your mattress clean for your health and comfort. Here are some effective tips to get rid of musty smells from your mattress.

## 1. Air the Mattress in Direct Sunlight

One of the most effective ways to remove a musty smell is by airing the mattress in direct sunlight. The sun's UV rays can kill the bacteria and mould that cause unpleasant odours. Air the mattress for 3-4 hours in the morning for the best results.

## 2. Use Bicarbonate of Soda

Bicarbonate of soda is a powerful natural odour absorber. Sprinkle bicarbonate of soda evenly over the entire surface of the mattress, leave it for 1-2 hours, then vacuum it clean. The bicarbonate of soda will absorb moisture and unpleasant odours from deep within the mattress.

## 3. Clean with a Natural Cleaning Solution

A 1:1 mixture of white vinegar and water can be used to clean stains and odours from the mattress. Spray this mixture onto the smelly area, leave it for 15 minutes, then wipe it with a clean cloth. Vinegar is effective at killing odour-causing bacteria.

## 4. Use Natural Fragrances

After cleaning, you can use natural fragrances such as lavender or tea tree essential oil mixed with water. Spray it onto the mattress to provide a fresh scent and natural antibacterial benefits.

## 5. Ensure Good Air Circulation

A damp mattress is a breeding ground for bacteria and mould. Ensure the bedroom has good air circulation by opening windows regularly. Use a dehumidifier if necessary to reduce humidity in the room.

## 6. Wash Bed Sheets and Pillowcases Regularly

Dirty bed sheets and pillowcases can cause unpleasant odours on the mattress. Wash sheets and pillowcases at least once a week with warm water and a detergent containing natural bleach.

## 7. Use a Mattress Protector

A mattress protector can prevent liquid spills and sweat from soaking into the mattress. Choose a breathable and easily washable mattress protector.

## 8. Replace the Mattress Periodically

An old and worn-out mattress tends to absorb more odours and dirt. Consider replacing your mattress every 5-7 years, depending on its condition.
