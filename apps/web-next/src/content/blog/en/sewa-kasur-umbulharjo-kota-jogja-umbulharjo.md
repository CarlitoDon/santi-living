## Determine the number of people and duration of use

The first step is to count the number of users, not just the number of items. For sleeping needs, consider who will be using the mattresses: children, adults, elderly guests, or visitors who need more space. For events, estimate the number of guests present at the same time, not the total number of invitations throughout the day.

Duration matters too. One night of use is very different from three to seven nights. The longer the duration, the more important it is to ensure items are comfortable, easy to move, and do not disrupt daily activities. If the schedule is not yet confirmed, share an estimated start and end date from the beginning so the team can help check availability.

## Check the space, access, and placement points

Before selecting items, assess the area to be used in practical terms. A precise measurement isn't necessary, but having a general picture helps — a narrow bedroom, living room, porch, garage, or accommodation area. For extra mattresses, think about walkways, door positions, and access to the bathroom. For fans, air coolers, or TVs, check the power outlets and the direction users will be facing.

If items will be used on an upper floor, a narrow alley, a boarding house, or a rental property with limited access, mention this from the start. Details like these help avoid miscalculations during delivery. The goal is simple: items arrive, can be placed immediately, and don't take up unnecessary time on-site.
