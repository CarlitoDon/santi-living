**Back pain after sleeping?** Perhaps your mattress is no longer fit for purpose. Renting the right mattress can be a solution while you wait for a new one.

## The Relationship Between Mattresses and Back Health

According to orthopaedic specialists, a good mattress must be:
- **Firm enough** to support the spine
- **Not too soft** so that the back does not sink
- **Flat on the surface** for even weight distribution

A saggy or dented mattress can cause:
- Lower back pain
- A stiff neck upon waking up
- Sleep disruption and poor quality of rest

## Criteria for a Good Rental Mattress for Your Back

### 1. High-Density Foam
High-density foam mattresses do not sag easily and provide consistent support for the spine.

### 2. Ideal Thickness
- **At least 15 cm** for adults
- **20 cm** for people weighing >80 kg

### 3. Medium Firm
Too firm makes the shoulders and hips sore. Too soft leaves the back unsupported. Medium firm is the best choice.

## Tips for Looking After Your Back When Renting a Mattress

1. **Use the right pillow** — A pillow that is too high causes neck pain
2. **Sleep on your back or side** — Avoid sleeping on your stomach
3. **Place the mattress on a flat surface** — Avoid uneven floors
